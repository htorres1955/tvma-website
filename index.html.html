Our Vision
Every child deserves a chance to discover their musical talent.
TVMA envisions a place where every child, regardless of socioeconomic background, has an opportunity to discover and develop their musical talents — enriching their lives and the community.
Our Difference
Made possible by community volunteers.
TVMA provides music theory and instruction to students in the Treasure Valley — all made possible by volunteers donating their time, knowledge, and equipment. Providing opportunities that are otherwise out of reach for local youth.
How We Teach
The TVMA Learning System
Every lesson follows a feedback loop borrowed from systems engineering — the same logic that drives great music and great technology.
Input → Process → Output → Feedback
Step 01
1
🎯
Learn
Start with the essentials. Strip away complexity and focus on what matters most right now. A single chord. A single concept. Done well.
Step 02
2
🎸
Apply
Put it into practice. Students play, build, and experiment with real instruments and real circuits. Theory meets reality every session.
Step 03
3
👂
Listen
Hear the feedback — from the music, from peers, from yourself. Music teaches you to listen before you react.
Step 04
4
🔁
Adjust
Refine and improve. The loop repeats — every cycle builds skill, confidence, and deeper understanding.
IPO Model This framework mirrors the Input → Process → Output → Feedback systems model used across engineering, science, and music — giving students a transferable mental model that works far beyond the classroom.
Our Programs
Two Pathways, One Mission
TVMA and TVEA work in tandem — music builds the foundation, electronics builds the future. Both programs are free to every student.
🎸
TVMA · Core Program
Guitar & Bass Program
Weekly group instruction in guitar, bass, and accordion. Students develop technique, music theory, and performance skills through a systems-thinking curriculum built from the ground up.
  • Guitar, bass, and accordion instruction
  • Music theory through the IPO framework
  • Small group sessions — up to 10 students
  • 60-minute weekly after-school sessions
  • Student progress reports for school accountability
Grades 6–11 After School Free to Students
⚡
TVEA · STEM Integration
Electronics Academy
A 12-week integrated music-and-electronics curriculum where students build a chromatic guitar tuner as the capstone project — connecting music theory to real engineering concepts.
  • 12-week structured electronics curriculum
  • Chromatic guitar tuner capstone build
  • Bilingual instruction — English & Spanish
  • Pathway to CWI Electronics Technology AAS
  • Connections to Micron, HP, and Idaho Power
STEM Pathway CWI Pipeline Bilingual Grant Funded
